# Output Templates

These templates produce LinkedIn-quality briefs. Use them as **structural guides, not fill-in-the-blank forms** — the analysis sections are written as flowing narrative prose; tables present scores only.

---

## Section 1: Per-Exec Product & Market Brief

File: `product-market-{slug}.md`

```markdown
# Clockchain Product & Market Profile: {Name}

> {Role} · interviewed by Head of AI Product · {ISO date}

## Metadata
- Type: product-market-discovery
- Interviewee: {Name} · {Role}
- Date: {timestamp}
- Final Ambiguity: {score}%
- Customer thesis (one line): {who they think the customer is}
- PMF read: {Strong demand evidence | Plausible but unproven | No evidence yet}

## Clarity Scorecard
| Dimension | Score | Level | One-line read |
|---|---|---|---|
| Customer Clarity | {s} | {Sharp/Forming/Hazy} | {…} |
| Product-Market Fit Evidence | {s} | {…} | {…} |
| Market & Industry Insight | {s} | {…} | {…} |
| Competitive Positioning | {s} | {…} | {…} |
| Product Goals & Vision | {s} | {…} | {…} |
| **Composite** | **{c}** | | **Ambiguity: {1-c}** |

## Executive Summary
3-4 sentences of narrative: who this exec thinks the customer is, whether they have real demand evidence, how they read the market, and the single biggest gap. Should stand alone.

## Customer Thesis
Narrative. WHO do they think the customer is — the vertical AND the buyer persona (title, budget owner)? State it in their words. Then: what's named and validated vs what's still a category. If they named a customer that conflicts with the pain they described, say so here — this is the most important finding.

## Product-Market Fit Read
Narrative. The pain they identified (how acute, who owns it). The pull/demand evidence — or the absence of it (interest ≠ demand; flag waitlists/VC-excitement as non-evidence). The wedge: the smallest thing a real customer would pay for.

## Market & Industry View
Narrative. How they read the industry, where value pools, the timing thesis (why now), named tailwinds. Distinguish a real thesis from "the market is huge."

## Competitive Positioning
Narrative. How they say Clockchain wins — vs named competitors (Sahara, 0G, Cronos, Story, Eliza) AND vs the status quo (block.timestamp / NTP / do-nothing). Whether the differentiation survives a competitor shipping the obvious version.

## Product Goals
Narrative. What they're trying to build in 12 months, the definition of success, the metric that would prove it.

## Assumptions Exposed
| # | Assumption (untested belief) | Evidence level | How to validate |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | {e.g. "platforms will pay for third-party identity"} | {none / anecdotal / data} | {specific test + signal} |

## Sharpest vs Haziest
- **Sharpest:** {dimension} — {why their thinking here is solid}
- **Haziest:** {dimension} — {the exposure; what they most need to resolve}

## Recommendations
- **Validate this month:** {1-3 concrete tests — e.g. "5 discovery calls with named compliance officers"}
- **Decide:** {the open customer/positioning decision they're avoiding}
- **Put to the team:** {questions to ask other execs to surface alignment}

## Interview Transcript
<details><summary>Full Q&A ({n} rounds)</summary>

### Round 1
**Q:** {question}
**A:** {answer}
**Scores:** Customer {c} · PMF {p} · Market {m} · Position {pos} · Goals {g} · Ambiguity {amb}%

...
</details>
```

## Section 4: Scoring Quick Reference

**Dimensions & weights:** Customer Clarity 25% · Product-Market Fit 25% · Market & Industry 20% · Competitive Positioning 15% · Product Goals 15%

**Composite:** `customer×0.25 + pmf×0.25 + market×0.20 + positioning×0.15 + goals×0.15`
**Ambiguity:** `1 − composite`

**Clarity levels:** Sharp ≥ 0.70 · Forming 0.40–0.69 · Hazy < 0.40

**Full per-dimension rubrics:** see knowledge.md → "The Five Product/Market Dimensions".

**Completion:** ambiguity ≤ 20%, OR Customer + PMF both ≥ 0.7 with others ≥ 0.5, OR user exit.
